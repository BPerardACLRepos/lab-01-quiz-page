"Adult" Kickball

a return to the playground

History of Kickball

Kickball was originally invented as a playground sport for soccer players who lacked the upper-body strength required for dodgeball. However, in the early 90's, Generation X ironically adopted it as an "adult" sport for people who hated adult sports, but liked alcohol; this would remain largely unchanged for the next ten years. In the year 2000, MTV made the choice to pivot from music to reality television, and hosted the first "adult" kickball tournament featuring people (mainly B-level celebrities and artists featured on "Jock Jams") who were actually trying to win. This would alter the trajectory of "adult" kickball, and usher in the age of the try-hard. Following this tournament, the spirit of kickball morphed from shrug-and-chug to a culture of "former athletes" who had knee injuries that prevented them from winning at flag football. Today, "adult" kickball is played by sub-par athletes at all levels of enthusiasm and drunkeness.

Rules of the Game

Kickball is a combination of America's most beloved (baseball) and most despised (football aka soccer) sports. The majority of the rules are from baseball, but the hard to hit and easy to throw ball is replaced with the recess red rubber ball that the batter attempts to kick. While most people play kickball with the slow, slightly bouncy schoolyard pitch, competitive leagues allow all kinds of trickery when getting the ball across the plate; the internet is rife with videos of hurt egos and butts from a swing-and-a-miss, look them up if you ever run out of cat videos. Throwing the ball at an offensive player in baseball probably starts a fight, but in "adult" kickball, this is considered an out and a way to shame your opponent. Other than that, it's straightforward enough for drunken adults to play, so you can pick it up pretty quickly.

Future of Kickball

At this point of time, kickball is played in leagues and tournaments across the nation. Unfortunately, that's about it. Unlike it's recess cousin, dodgeball, no other country has taken on "adult" kickball as a sport, and nothing is in the works to change that. Viral videos featured on ESPN have lead to interest from investors like Mark Cuban, however, when push came to shove, ESPN 8 (The Ocho) decided that "Cup Stacking" was a better choice. Another push for legitimization may be made at some point, but the ego of the "adult" kickball community will have to recover first.
